# Number System

## Important Formulae

(i)  $(a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + 2ab$

(ii) $(a - b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab$

(iii) $(a + b)^2 + (a - b)^2 = 2(a^2 + b^2)$

(iv) $(a + b)^2 - (a - b)^2 = 4ab$

(v)  $(a + b)^3 = a^3 + b^3 + 3ab(a + b)$

(vi) $(a - b)^3 = a^3 - b^3 - 3ab(a - b)$

(vii) $a^2 - b^2 = (a + b)(a - b)$

(viii) $(a + b + c)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2(ab + bc + ca)$

(ix) $a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 + b^2 - ab)$

(x)  $a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + b^2 + ab)$

(xi) $a^3 + b^3 + c^3 - 3abc = (a + b + c)(a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - ab - bc - ca)$

(xii) If $a + b + c = 0$, then $a^3 + b^3 + c^3 = 3abc$

# Tests of Divisibility

## Divisibility By 2

A number is divisible by 2 if its unit digit is any of 0, 2, 4, 6, 8.

Ex. 58694 is divisible by 2, while 86945 is not divisible by 2.

---

## Divisibility By 3

A number is divisible by 3 only when the sum of its digits is divisible by 3.

Ex.

(i) In the number 695421, the sum of digits = 27, which is divisible by 3.  
∴ 695421 is divisible by 3.

(ii) In the number 948653, the sum of digits = 35, which is not divisible by 3.  
∴ 948653 is not divisible by 3.

---

## Divisibility By 9

A number is divisible by 9 only when the sum of its digits is divisible by 9.

Ex.

(i) In the number 246591, the sum of digits = 27, which is divisible by 9.  
∴ 246591 is divisible by 9.

(ii) In the number 734519, the sum of digits = 29, which is not divisible by 9.  
∴ 734519 is not divisible by 9.

---

## Divisibility By 4

A number is divisible by 4 if the number formed by its last two digits is divisible by 4.

Ex.

(i) 6879376 is divisible by 4, since 76 is divisible by 4.

(ii) 496138 is not divisible by 4, since 38 is not divisible by 4.

---

## Divisibility By 8

A number is divisible by 8 if the number formed by its last three digits is divisible by 8.

Ex.

(i) In the number 16789352, the number formed by last 3 digits, namely 352 is divisible by 8.  
∴ 16789352 is divisible by 8.

(ii) In the number 576484, the number formed by last 3 digits, namely 484 is not divisible by 8.  
∴ 576484 is not divisible by 8.

---

## Divisibility By 10

A number is divisible by 10 only when its unit digit is 0.

Ex.

(i) 7849320 is divisible by 10, since its unit digit is 0.

(ii) 678405 is not divisible by 10, since its unit digit is not 0.

---

## Divisibility By 5

A number is divisible by 5 only when its unit digit is 0 or 5.

Ex.

(i) Each of the numbers 76895 and 68790 is divisible by 5.

---

## Divisibility By 11

A number is divisible by 11 if the difference between the sum of its digits at odd places and the sum of its digits at even places is either 0 or a number divisible by 11.

Ex.

(i) Consider the number 29435417.

(Sum of its digits at odd places) - (Sum of its digits at even places)

= (7 + 4 + 3 + 9) - (1 + 5 + 4 + 2)

= (23 - 12) = 11, which is divisible by 11.

∴ 29435417 is divisible by 11.

(ii) Consider the number 57463822.

(Sum of its digits at odd places) - (Sum of its digits at even places)

= (2 + 8 + 6 + 7) - (2 + 3 + 4 + 5)

= (23 - 14) = 9, which is not divisible by 11.

∴ 57463822 is not divisible by 11.

---

## Divisibility By 25

A number is divisible by 25 if the number formed by its last two digits is either 00 or divisible by 25.

Ex.

(i) In the number 63875, the number formed by last 2 digits, namely 75 is divisible by 25.  
∴ 63875 is divisible by 25.

(ii) In the number 96445, the number formed by last 2 digits, namely 45 is not divisible by 25.  
∴ 96445 is not divisible by 25.


# Example Problems

## 1. Divisibility Problem

x is a positive integer such that $(x^2 + 12)$ is exactly divisible by x. Find all the possible values of x.

### Solution

$$
\frac{x^2 + 12}{x} = x + \frac{12}{x}
$$

For this expression to be an integer, $\frac{12}{x}$ must also be an integer.

So, x must be a divisor of 12.

The positive divisors of 12 are:

- 1
- 2
- 3
- 4
- 6
- 12

Hence, the possible values of x are:

1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12

---

## 2. Least Number to be Added

Find the smallest number to be added to 1000 so that 45 divides the sum exactly.

### Solution

On dividing 1000 by 45, we get 10 as remainder.

$$
\text{Number to be added} = (45 - 10) = 35
$$

---

## 3. Unit Digit Problem

Find the unit's digit in the product:

$$
(2467)^{153} \times (341)^{72}
$$

### Solution

Clearly, unit's digit in the given product = unit's digit in:

$$
7^{153} \times 1^{72}
$$

Now,

$$
7^4
$$

gives unit digit 1.

$$
7^{152}
$$

gives unit digit 1.

$$
7^{153}
$$

gives unit digit:

$$
(1 \times 7) = 7
$$

Also,

$$
1^{72}
$$

gives unit digit 1.

Hence, unit digit in the product = (7 × 1) = 7.

# H.C.F. and L.C.M. of Numbers

## I. Factors and Multiples

If a number a divides another number b exactly, we say that a is a factor of b. In this case, b is called a multiple of a.

---

## II. Highest Common Factor (H.C.F.) or Greatest Common Measure (G.C.M.) or Greatest Common Divisor (G.C.D.)

The H.C.F. of two or more than two numbers is the greatest number that divides each of them exactly.

There are two methods of finding the H.C.F. of a given set of numbers:

### Factorization Method

Express each one of the given numbers as the product of prime factors.

The product of least powers of common prime factors gives H.C.F.

### Division Method

Suppose we have to find the H.C.F. of two given numbers.

- Divide the larger number by the smaller one.
- Divide the divisor by the remainder.
- Repeat the process till remainder becomes zero.

The last divisor is the required H.C.F.

### Finding the H.C.F. of More Than Two Numbers

Suppose we have to find the H.C.F. of three numbers.

Then,

H.C.F. of:

[(H.C.F. of any two) and (the third number)]

gives the H.C.F. of three given numbers.

Similarly, the H.C.F. of more than three numbers may be obtained.

---

## III. Least Common Multiple (L.C.M.)

The least number which is exactly divisible by each one of the given numbers is called their L.C.M.

### Factorization Method of Finding L.C.M.

Resolve each one of the given numbers into a product of prime factors.

Then, L.C.M. is the product of highest powers of all the factors.

### Common Division Method (Short-cut Method) of Finding L.C.M.

- Arrange the given numbers in a row in any order.
- Divide by a number which divides exactly at least two of the given numbers.
- Carry forward the numbers which are not divisible.
- Repeat the process till no two of the numbers are divisible by the same number except 1.

The product of the divisors and the undivided numbers is the required L.C.M. of the given numbers.

---

## IV. Product of Two Numbers

Product of two numbers = Product of their H.C.F. and L.C.M.

---

## V. Co-primes

Two numbers are said to be co-primes if their H.C.F. is 1.

---

## VI. H.C.F. and L.C.M. of Fractions

### H.C.F. of Fractions

$$
\text{H.C.F.} =
\frac{\text{H.C.F. of Numerators}}
{\text{L.C.M. of Denominators}}
$$

### L.C.M. of Fractions

$$
\text{L.C.M.} =
\frac{\text{L.C.M. of Numerators}}
{\text{H.C.F. of Denominators}}
$$

---

## VII. H.C.F. and L.C.M. of Decimal Fractions

In given numbers, make the same number of decimal places by annexing zeros in some numbers, if necessary.

Considering these numbers without decimal point, find H.C.F. or L.C.M. as the case may be.

Now, in the result, mark off as many decimal places as are there in each of the given numbers.

---

## VIII. Comparison of Fractions

Find the L.C.M. of the denominators of the given fractions.

Convert each of the fractions into an equivalent fraction with L.C.M. as the denominator, by multiplying both the numerator and denominator by the same number.

The resultant fraction with the greatest numerator is the greatest.

# Ratio and Proportion

## IMPORTANT CONCEPTS

## I. Ratio

The ratio of two quantities a and b in the same units is the fraction:

$$
\frac{a}{b}
$$

written as:

$$
a : b
$$

In the ratio \(a : b\),

- a is called the antecedent (first term)
- b is called the consequent (second term)

### Example

$$
5 : 9 = \frac{5}{9}
$$

where antecedent = 5 and consequent = 9.

### Rule

Multiplying or dividing each term of a ratio by the same non-zero number does not change the ratio.

### Example

$$
4 : 5 = 8 : 10 = 12 : 15
$$

$$
4 : 6 = 2 : 3
$$

---

## II. Proportion

The equality of two ratios is called a proportion.

If

$$
a : b = c : d
$$

then we write:

$$
a : b :: c : d
$$

Here,

- a and d are called extremes
- b and c are called mean terms

### Property

Product of means = Product of extremes

$$
b \times c = a \times d
$$

---

## III. Proportion Types

### (i) Fourth Proportional

If

$$
a : b = c : d
$$

then d is the fourth proportional to a, b, c.

---

### (ii) Third Proportional

If

$$
a : b = b : c
$$

then c is the third proportional to a and b.

---

### (iii) Mean Proportional

Mean proportional between a and b:

$$
\sqrt{ab}
$$

---

## IV. Ratios

### (i) Comparison of Ratios

$$
\frac{a}{b} > \frac{c}{d}
\iff
ad > bc
$$

---

### (ii) Compounded Ratio

The compounded ratio of:

$$
(a : b), (c : d), (e : f)
$$

is:

$$
(ace : bdf)
$$

---

## V. Special Ratios

### (i) Duplicate Ratio

Duplicate ratio of:

$$
(a : b) = (a^2 : b^2)
$$

---

### (ii) Sub-duplicate Ratio

Sub-duplicate ratio of:

$$
(a : b) = (\sqrt{a} : \sqrt{b})
$$

---

### (iii) Triplicate Ratio

Triplicate ratio of:

$$
(a : b) = (a^3 : b^3)
$$

---

### (iv) Sub-triplicate Ratio

Sub-triplicate ratio of:

$$
(a : b) =
(a^{1/3} : b^{1/3})
$$

---

### (v) Componendo and Dividendo

If

$$
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}
$$

then

$$
\frac{a + b}{a - b}
=
\frac{c + d}{c - d}
$$

---

## VI. Variation

### (i) Direct Variation

$$
x \propto y
$$

$$
x = ky
$$

where \(k\) is a constant.

---

### (ii) Inverse Variation

$$
x \propto \frac{1}{y}
$$

$$
xy = k
$$

---

## VII. Replacement (Mixture Concept)

If a container has x units of liquid and y units are replaced each time,

then after n operations, the quantity of pure liquid left is:

$$
x \times
\left(
1 - \frac{y}{x}
\right)^n
$$

# Averages

## IMPORTANT FACTS AND FORMULAE

## I. Average Formula

$$
\text{Average}
=
\frac{\text{Sum of observations}}
{\text{Number of observations}}
$$

---

## II. Average Speed (Equal Distances)

If a man covers a certain distance at \(x\) km/h and an equal distance at \(y\) km/h, then the average speed for the whole journey is:

$$
\text{Average Speed}
=
\frac{2xy}{x + y}
\text{ km/h}
$$

# Profit and Loss

## IMPORTANT FACTS AND FORMULAE

### Cost Price (C.P.)

The price at which an article is purchased.

### Selling Price (S.P.)

The price at which an article is sold.

### Profit or Gain

If

$$
S.P. > C.P.
$$

---

### Loss

If

$$
S.P. < C.P.
$$

---

## I. Gain

$$
\text{Gain} = S.P. - C.P.
$$

---

## II. Loss

$$
\text{Loss} = C.P. - S.P.
$$

---

## III.

Gain or Loss is always calculated on C.P.

---

## IV. Gain%

$$
\text{Gain\%}
=
\left(
\frac{\text{Gain}}{\text{C.P.}}
\right)
\times 100
$$

---

## V. Loss%

$$
\text{Loss\%}
=
\left(
\frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{C.P.}}
\right)
\times 100
$$

---

## VI. Selling Price Formula (Gain)

$$
S.P.
=
\left(
\frac{100 + \text{Gain\%}}{100}
\right)
\times C.P.
$$

---

## VII. Selling Price Formula (Loss)

$$
S.P.
=
\left(
\frac{100 - \text{Loss\%}}{100}
\right)
\times C.P.
$$

---

## VIII. Cost Price Formula (Gain)

$$
C.P.
=
\left(
\frac{100}{100 + \text{Gain\%}}
\right)
\times S.P.
$$

---

## IX. Cost Price Formula (Loss)

$$
C.P.
=
\left(
\frac{100}{100 - \text{Loss\%}}
\right)
\times S.P.
$$

---

## X.

If gain is \(x\%\), then

$$
S.P. = (100 + x)\% \text{ of C.P.}
$$

---

## XI.

If loss is \(x\%\), then

$$
S.P. = (100 - x)\% \text{ of C.P.}
$$

---

## XII.

When two similar items are sold, one at \(x\%\) gain and the other at \(x\%\) loss, then

$$
\text{Net Loss\%}
=
\frac{x^2}{100}
$$

---

## XIII.

If a trader sells at cost price but uses false weight, then

$$
\text{Gain\%}
=
\left(
\frac{\text{Error}}
{\text{True Value} - \text{Error}}
\right)
\times 100
$$

---

## XIV.

If a trader claims \(x\%\) profit but uses \(y\%\) less weight, then

$$
\text{Gain\%}
=
\left(
\frac{x + y}{100 - y}
\right)
\times 100
$$

---

## XV.

If a trader claims \(x\%\) loss but uses \(y\%\) less weight, then

$$
\text{Net Gain/Loss\%}
=
\left(
\frac{y - x}{100 - y}
\right)
\times 100
$$

Positive result → Gain

Negative result → Loss

# Simple and Compound Interest

## IMPORTANT FACTS AND FORMULAE

# Simple Interest

## I. Principal (P)

The money borrowed or lent for a certain period is called the principal.

---

## II. Interest

The extra money paid for using someone else’s money is called interest.

---

## III. Simple Interest (S.I.)

If interest is calculated uniformly on the principal for the entire time, it is called simple interest.

Let:

\(P\) = Principal

\(R\) = Rate of interest per annum (%)

\(T\) = Time (in years)

---

## (i) Simple Interest

$$
S.I.
=
\frac{P \times R \times T}{100}
$$

---

## (ii) Principal

$$
P
=
\frac{S.I. \times 100}{R \times T}
$$

---

## (iii) Rate

$$
R
=
\frac{S.I. \times 100}{P \times T}
$$

---

## (iv) Time

$$
T
=
\frac{S.I. \times 100}{P \times R}
$$

---

## (v) Amount

$$
A = P + S.I.
$$

# Compound Interest

Let:

\(P\) = Principal

\(R\) = Rate of interest per annum (%)

\(n\) = Time (in years)

---

## I. Compounded Annually

$$
A
=
P
\left(
1 + \frac{R}{100}
\right)^n
$$

---

## II. Compounded Half-Yearly

$$
A
=
P
\left(
1 + \frac{(R/2)}{100}
\right)^{2n}
$$

---

## III. Compounded Quarterly

$$
A
=
P
\left(
1 + \frac{(R/4)}{100}
\right)^{4n}
$$

---

## IV. Fractional Time

When interest is compounded annually but time is fractional (e.g., 3½ years):

$$
A
=
P
\left(
1 + \frac{R}{100}
\right)^3
\left(
1 + \frac{(R/2)}{100}
\right)
$$

---

## V. Different Rates for Different Years

If rates are:

\(R_1\%\), \(R_2\%\), \(R_3\%\)

then

$$
A
=
P
\left(
1 + \frac{R_1}{100}
\right)
\left(
1 + \frac{R_2}{100}
\right)
\left(
1 + \frac{R_3}{100}
\right)
$$

---

## VI. Present Worth

Present Worth (\(P\)) of ₹x due after n years:

$$
P
=
\frac{x}
{
\left(
1 + \frac{R}{100}
\right)^n
}
$$

=
\frac{S.I. \times 100}{P \times R}
$$

$$
T
=
\frac{P \times 100}{P \times 10}
=
10 \text{ years}
$$

Therefore, required time = 10 years.

## Ex. 2

A and B can do a piece of work in 12 days. B and C together can do it in 15 days. If A is twice as good a workman as C, find in what time B alone can do it.

### Solution

(A + B)’s 1 day work = 1/12

(B + C)’s 1 day work = 1/15

Given: A is twice as efficient as C

⇒ A’s 1 day work = 2 × (C’s 1 day work)

So,

(A + B)’s work = B + 2C

⇒ B + 2C = 1/12 ...(i)

⇒ B + C = 1/15 ...(ii)

Subtract (ii) from (i):

(B + 2C) − (B + C) = 1/12 − 1/15

⇒ C’s 1 day work = (5 − 4)/60 = 1/60

Now from (ii):

B + C = 1/15

⇒ B = 1/15 − 1/60

⇒ B = (4 − 1)/60 = 3/60 = 1/20

Therefore, B alone can complete the work in 20 days
