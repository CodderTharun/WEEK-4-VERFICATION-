# Number Systems

## Introduction

Quantitative ability forms the backbone of competitive examinations. This week focuses on number systems and basic arithmetic operations that serve as the foundation for all advanced concepts.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Number Classification

Numbers are classified as:
- **Natural Numbers**: 1, 2, 3, ... (positive integers)
- **Whole Numbers**: 0, 1, 2, 3, ... (natural numbers plus zero)
- **Integers**: ..., -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, ... (positive and negative whole numbers)
- **Rational Numbers**: Numbers that can be expressed as p/q (where q ≠ 0)
- **Irrational Numbers**: Numbers that cannot be expressed as p/q (e.g., π, √2)
- **Real Numbers**: Combination of rational and irrational numbers

### 2. Properties of Numbers

**Divisibility Rules:**
- Divisible by 2: Last digit is even
- Divisible by 3: Sum of digits is divisible by 3
- Divisible by 5: Last digit is 0 or 5
- Divisible by 9: Sum of digits is divisible by 9

**Prime and Composite:**
- Prime Numbers: Numbers greater than 1 that have no divisors other than 1 and itself
- Composite Numbers: Numbers that have more than two divisors

### 3. Basic Arithmetic Operations

$$\text{Sum} = a + b$$
$$\text{Difference} = a - b$$
$$\text{Product} = a \times b$$
$$\text{Quotient} = \frac{a}{b}$$

### 4. Percentage and Ratio

**Percentage**: A ratio expressed as a fraction of 100
$$\text{Percentage} = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100$$

**Ratio**: Comparison of two quantities
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{a}{b} \text{ or } a:b$$
