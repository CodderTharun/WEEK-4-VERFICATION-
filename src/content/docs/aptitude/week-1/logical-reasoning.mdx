#Series and Sequences

## Introduction

Logical reasoning tests your ability to think critically and identify patterns. This week focuses on understanding sequences and series that form the foundation of logical thinking.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Number Series

A sequence of numbers following a specific pattern or rule.

**Common Patterns:**
- Arithmetic Sequence: Each term differs by a constant value (d)
  - Example: 2, 5, 8, 11, 14... (common difference = 3)
  - Formula: $$a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$$

- Geometric Sequence: Each term is multiplied by a constant ratio (r)
  - Example: 2, 6, 18, 54... (common ratio = 3)
  - Formula: $$a_n = a_1 \times r^{n-1}$$

### 2. Letter Series

Patterns based on the alphabetical order of letters.

**Examples:**
- A, C, E, G, I... (every alternate letter)
- Z, X, V, T, R... (every second letter backward)

### 3. Mixed Series

Combinations of numbers and letters following specific patterns.

### 4. Pattern Recognition Types

- **Addition/Subtraction Pattern**: Fixed increment or decrement
- **Multiplication/Division Pattern**: Fixed multiplier or divisor
- **Combination Pattern**: Mix of operations
- **Fibonacci Pattern**: Each term is sum of previous two terms

## This Week's Topics

1. Identifying arithmetic sequences and finding missing terms
2. Recognizing geometric progressions
3. Letter series pattern analysis
4. Mixed number-letter series
5. Complex pattern identification

## Practice Problems

1. Find the missing number: 3, 7, 11, 15, ?, 23
2. Complete the series: 2, 4, 8, 16, ?
3. Letter series: A, D, G, J, ?, P
4. Mixed series: 1A, 2B, 3C, ?, 5E
5. Fibonacci-like: 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, ?

## Key Formulas

**Arithmetic Progression (AP):**
$$S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a + (n-1)d)$$

**Geometric Progression (GP):**
$$S_n = a \times \frac{r^n - 1}{r - 1}$$

## Tips for Success

- Always identify the pattern first before solving
- Look for differences or ratios between consecutive terms
- Consider multiple possibilities before concluding
- Practice different pattern types daily
- Build a library of common patterns

Remember: The key to series problems is pattern recognition!
