# Reading Comprehension Basics

## Introduction

Verbal reasoning tests your understanding of language, vocabulary, and comprehension skills. This week focuses on building strong reading comprehension foundations.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Reading Comprehension Strategies

**Active Reading:**
- Skim the passage first to understand the main idea
- Identify key themes and supporting details
- Note important dates, names, and statistics
- Understand the author's tone and perspective

**Question Types:**
- Main Idea Questions
- Detailed/Factual Questions
- Inference Questions
- Vocabulary/Context Questions
- Author's Purpose/Tone Questions

### 2. Vocabulary Enhancement

**Word Categories:**
- Synonyms: Words with similar meanings
- Antonyms: Words with opposite meanings
- Homonyms: Words spelled the same but different meanings
- Homophones: Words that sound the same

**Common Word Roots:**
- Latin roots (e.g., "morph" = shape)
- Greek roots (e.g., "bio" = life)
- Prefixes (e.g., "un-", "re-", "pre-")
- Suffixes (e.g., "-tion", "-able", "-ment")

### 3. Comprehension Techniques

**Before Reading:**
- Preview the title and any visuals
- Predict what the passage might contain

**During Reading:**
- Focus on understanding, not speed initially
- Mark key sentences and ideas
- Link new information to what you know

**After Reading:**
- Summarize the main points
- Answer practice questions
- Review any unknown vocabulary

## This Week's Topics

1. Effective reading strategies and techniques
2. Identifying main ideas and supporting details
3. Understanding author's tone and perspective
4. Building vocabulary through context
5. Answering comprehension questions accurately

## Practice Exercise

**Passage:** Technology has revolutionized education, making learning more accessible. Students now access courses globally, breaking geographical barriers. However, this digital shift requires strong self-discipline and intrinsic motivation.

**Questions:**
1. What is the main idea of the passage?
2. What benefit does digital learning provide?
3. What challenge comes with online education?
4. What is the author's tone?

## Key Tips

- Read passages multiple times if needed
- Understand the question before searching for the answer
- Use context clues for unknown words
- Practice different passage types
- Learn one word root daily

## Vocabulary Building

- Practice: 5 new words daily
- Create flashcards with meanings and examples
- Use words in sentences
- Review previously learned words weekly
- Focus on word families and roots

Build strong reading habits for long-term success!
