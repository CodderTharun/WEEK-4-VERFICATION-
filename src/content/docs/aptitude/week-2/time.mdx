# Time, Speed and Distance

## A. Formulas

### 1. Basic Formula

```text
Distance = Speed × Time
Speed = Distance / Time
Time = Distance / Speed
```

### 2. Conversion of Speed

```text
x km/hr = (x × 5/18) m/sec
```

### 3. Conversion of Speed

```text
x m/sec = (x × 18/5) km/hr
```

### 4. Ratio of Speeds and Times

If the ratio of speeds of A and B is `a : b`, then the ratio of times taken by them to cover the same distance is:

```text
b : a or 1/a : 1/b
```

### 5. Average Speed Formula

If a man covers a certain distance at `x km/hr` and an equal distance at `y km/hr`, then average speed for the whole journey:

```text
Average speed = 2xy / (x + y) km/hr
```

### 6. Relative Speed (Same Direction)

If two persons move in the same direction at `u m/s` and `v m/s` respectively, where `u > v`, then:

```text
Relative speed = (u − v) m/s
```

### 7. Relative Speed (Opposite Direction)

If two persons move in opposite directions at `u m/s` and `v m/s` respectively, then:

```text
Relative speed = (u + v) m/s
```

### 8. Crossing Each Other

If two persons A and B start at the same time in opposite directions from two points and after crossing each other they complete their journeys in `a` and `b` hours respectively, then:

```text
A’s speed : B’s speed = √b : √a
```

### 9. Train Crossing a Pole or Man

Time taken by a train of length `l` metres to pass a pole, standing man or signal post:

```text
Time = l / Speed
```

### 10. Time Taken by Two Trains to Cross Each Other

If lengths of trains are `a` metres and `b` metres:

```text
Moving in opposite directions:
Time = (a + b) / (u + v)

Moving in same direction:
Time = (a + b) / (u − v)
```

# Boats and Streams – Important Formulas

## Downstream and Upstream

In water transport:

- The direction along the stream is called downstream.
- The direction against the stream is called upstream.

## Basic Formulas

If:

```text
Speed of boat in still water = u km/hr
Speed of stream = v km/hr
```

Then:

```text
Speed downstream = (u + v) km/hr
Speed upstream = (u − v) km/hr
```

## Finding Boat Speed and Stream Speed

If:

```text
Speed downstream = a km/hr
Speed upstream = b km/hr
```

Then:

```text
Speed in still water = (a + b)/2 km/hr
Rate of stream = (a − b)/2 km/hr
```

# Example Problems

## Example 1

### Question

How many minutes does Aditya take to cover a distance of `400 m`, if he runs at a speed of `20 km/hr`?

### Solution

```text
Aditya’s speed = 20 km/hr
= (20 × 5/18) m/sec
= 50/9 m/sec

Time taken to cover 400 m
= Distance / Speed
= 400 ÷ (50/9)
= 400 × 9/50
= 72 seconds
= 1 minute 12 seconds
```

---

## Example 2

### Question

A man can row `6 km/hr` in still water. It takes him twice as long to row up as to row down the river. Find the rate of stream.

### Solution

```text
Let man’s rate upstream = x km/hr

Then, his rate downstream = 2x km/hr

Rate in still water
= (Upstream speed + Downstream speed) / 2
= (x + 2x) / 2
= 3x/2 km/hr

But, rate in still water = 6 km/hr

Therefore,
3x/2 = 6
3x = 12
x = 4

Therefore,
Rate upstream = 4 km/hr
Rate downstream = 8 km/hr

Rate of stream
= (Downstream speed − Upstream speed) / 2
= (8 − 4) / 2
= 2 km/hr
```

# Permutations and Combinations

## I. Factorial

Let `n` be a positive integer. Then factorial `n` is denoted by `n!` and is defined as:

```text
n! = n × (n − 1) × (n − 2) × … × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
```

### Examples

```text
4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24
5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120
```

### Note

```text
0! = 1
```

---

## II. Permutations

### (i) Definition

The different arrangements of a given number of things by taking some or all at a time are called permutations.

### Examples

All permutations made with letters `a, b, c` by taking two at a time are:

```text
(ab, ba, ac, ca, bc, cb)
```

All permutations made with letters `a, b, c` by taking three at a time are:

```text
(abc, acb, bac, bca, cab, cba)
```

---

### (ii) Number of Permutations

Number of permutations of `n` things taken `r` at a time:

```text
nPr = n! / (n − r)!
= n × (n − 1) × (n − 2) × … × (n − r + 1)
```

### Examples

```text
8P2 = 8 × 7 = 56
7P3 = 7 × 6 × 5 = 210
```

---

### (iii) Permutations of n Things Taken All at a Time

```text
Number of permutations = n!
```

---

### (iv) Permutations of Similar Objects

If there are `n` objects of which:

```text
p1 are alike of one kind
p2 are alike of another kind
p3 are alike of third kind
pr are alike of rth kind
```

such that:

```text
p1 + p2 + p3 + … + pr = n
```

then number of permutations:

```text
n! / (p1! × p2! × p3! × … × pr!)
```

# III. Combinations

## (i) Definition

Each of the different groups or selections formed by taking some or all objects at a time is called a combination.

### Examples

Out of three boys `A, B, C` selecting two:

```text
(AB, BC, CA)
```

### Note

```text
AB and BA represent the same combination
```

The only combination of `A, B, C` taken all at a time is:

```text
ABC
```

Groups of two from `A, B, C, D` are:

```text
AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD
```

### Important Note

```text
AB and BA are different permutations,
but they represent the same combination
```

---

## (ii) Number of Combinations

Number of combinations of `n` things taken `r` at a time:

```text
nCr = n! / [r! × (n − r)!]
```

### Examples

```text
8C3 = (8 × 7 × 6) / (3 × 2 × 1)
= 56

16C3 = (16 × 15 × 14) / (3 × 2 × 1)
= 560
```

---

## (iii) Important Properties

```text
nCn = 1
nC0 = 1
nCr = nC(n − r)
```

## Example 10

### Question

In how many different ways can the letters of the word `"DIGEST"` be arranged so that the vowels are never together?

### Solution

```text
The word “DIGEST” contains 6 different letters

Vowels are: I, E

First, find the number of arrangements in which the vowels are together

Treat IE as one letter

Then the letters become:
D, G, S, T, (IE)

Thus, there are 5 letters

Number of arrangements
= 5!
= 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
= 120

The vowels I and E can be arranged among themselves in 2! ways
= 2

Therefore,
Number of arrangements with vowels together
= 120 × 2
= 240

Now, Total number of arrangements of the word “DIGEST”
= 6!
= 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
= 720

Therefore,
Number of arrangements in which vowels are never together
= 720 − 240
= 480
```

Hence, the required number of arrangements is `480`.

# Basic Concepts of Probability

Probability is defined as the likelihood of the occurrence of any event. It gives a numerical value to the chance or likelihood of something happening.

Probability is generally denoted by `P(E)`, where `E` represents the event.

It is expressed as a number between `0` and `1`:

```text
0 → impossible event
1 → certain event
0 < P(E) < 1 → partial chance
```

---

## Formula for Probability

```text
Probability of Event P(E)
= [Number of Favorable Outcomes] / [Total Number of Outcomes]
```

The probability of an event `E`, denoted by `P(E)`, is a number between `0` and `1` that represents the likelihood of `E` occurring.

```text
If P(E) = 0 → impossible
If P(E) = 1 → certain
If 0 < P(E) < 1 → possible but not guaranteed
```

---

## Basic Probability Rules

### Addition Rule

```text
P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B)
```

### Multiplication Rule for Independent Events

```text
P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)
```

### Complement Rule

```text
P(A ′) = 1 - P(A)
```

# Probability Examples

## Example 1

### Question

There are `6` pillows in a bed, `3` are red, `2` are yellow, and `1` is blue. What is the probability of randomly picking a yellow pillow?

### Solution

```text
Probability
= Number of yellow pillows / Total pillows
= 2/6
= 1/3
```

# Linear Equations

A linear equation is one in which the highest power of a variable like `x` or `y` is `1`.

Examples:

```text
x + 10
y + 1
```

This means the graph of the equation is a straight line.

### Examples

```text
Perimeter of square:
P = 4s

Perimeter of rectangle:
P = 2l + 2w

Distance at constant speed:
d = vt
```

# Quadratic Equations

## What is a Quadratic Formula?

The quadratic formula is used to find the x-intercepts of a quadratic equation.

Where:

```text
a ≠ 0
```

If `a = 0`, then the equation becomes linear.

`a`, `b`, and `c` are called:

```text
Quadratic coefficient
Linear coefficient
Constant term
```
