# Inference and Critical Reading

## Introduction

This week advances your reading skills by teaching inference - the ability to read between the lines and understand implied meanings.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Understanding Inference

**Inference = Logical Conclusion based on given information**

- Explicit information: Directly stated in text
- Implicit information: Between the lines, not directly stated
- Inference: Your logical conclusion from evidence

### 2. Types of Inferences

**Factual Inference:**
- Based on specific data or facts
- Example: "It was 40°C yesterday" → Today might be hot too

**Logical Inference:**
- Based on cause and effect
- Example: "All plants need water" + "Rose is a plant" → Rose needs water

**Contextual Inference:**
- Based on surrounding information and context
- Example: From a medical article discussing symptoms

**Emotional/Tonal Inference:**
- Understanding author's attitude
- Example: Sarcasm, criticism, praise

### 3. Reading Between the Lines

**Techniques:**
- Look for supporting evidence
- Consider what's NOT said
- Understand author's perspective
- Connect ideas logically
- Question your assumptions

### 4. Critical Reading Strategies

**Ask These Questions:**
- What is the main idea?
- What evidence supports it?
- Are there alternatives?
- What assumptions is the author making?
- Is the logic sound?

## This Week's Topics

1. Making valid inferences from text
2. Distinguishing fact from inference
3. Identifying author's assumptions
4. Critical evaluation of arguments
5. Bias recognition and perspective analysis

## Practice Exercise

**Passage:**
"Renewable energy sources are increasingly competitive economically. Solar installations have become 89% cheaper over the past decade. Wind energy now provides substantial power in many countries."

**Inference Questions:**
1. What is likely to be the author's view on renewable energy?
2. Why might solar become even more popular?
3. What does 89% cheaper suggest about the future?
4. Is the author biased toward renewables? Why?

## Key Principles

- Inferences must be supported by evidence
- Don't make wild guesses
- Distinguish between "probably true" and "definitely true"
- Consider the author's perspective
- Look for logical consistency

## Tips for Success

- Read passages at least twice
- Underline key supporting details
- Ask "why" questions while reading
- Practice making logical connections
- Review inference mistakes carefully
- Build reading comprehension over time

Develop strong inference skills for critical thinking!
