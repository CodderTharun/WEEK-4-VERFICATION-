## Boats and Streams 

### A. Downstream and Upstream

In water transport:

- The direction along the stream is called downstream.
- The direction against the stream is called upstream.

### B. Basic Formulas

If:

Speed of boat in still water = u km/hr
Speed of stream = v km/hr


Then:


Speed downstream = (u + v) km/hr
Speed upstream = (u − v) km/hr


### C. Finding Boat Speed and Stream Speed

If:

Speed downstream = a km/hr
Speed upstream = b km/hr

Then:

Speed in still water = (a + b)/2 km/hr
Rate of stream = (a − b)/2 km/hr

---

### D. Example Problems


```Example
1. How many minutes does Aditya take to cover a distance of `400 m`, if he runs at a speed of `20 km/hr`?

Solution:
Aditya’s speed = 20 km/hr
= (20 × 5/18) m/sec
= 50/9 m/sec

Time taken to cover 400 m
= Distance / Speed
= 400 ÷ (50/9)
= 400 × 9/50
= 72 seconds
= 1 minute 12 seconds


2. A man can row `6 km/hr` in still water. It takes him twice as long to row up as to row down the river. Find the rate of stream.

Solution:
Let man’s rate upstream = x km/hr
Then, his rate downstream = 2x km/hr

Rate in still water
= (Upstream speed + Downstream speed) / 2
= (x + 2x) / 2
= 3x/2 km/hr

But, rate in still water = 6 km/hr

Therefore,
3x/2 = 6
3x = 12
x = 4

Therefore,
Rate upstream = 4 km/hr
Rate downstream = 8 km/hr

Rate of stream
= (Downstream speed − Upstream speed) / 2
= (8 − 4) / 2
= 2 km/hr
```

## Permutations and Combinations

### A. Factorial

Let n be a positive integer. Then factorial n is denoted by n! and is defined as:


n! = n × (n − 1) × (n − 2) × … × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1


##### **Examples**:

4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24
5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120


##### Note

0! = 1


---

### B. Permutations

#### 1. Definition

The different arrangements of a given number of things by taking some or all at a time are called permutations.

##### Examples:

All permutations made with letters a, b, c by taking two at a time are:

(ab, ba, ac, ca, bc, cb)

All permutations made with letters a, b, c by taking three at a time are:

(abc, acb, bac, bca, cab, cba)

---

#### 2. Number of Permutations

Number of permutations of n things taken r at a time:

nPr = n! / (n − r)!
= n × (n − 1) × (n − 2) × … × (n − r + 1)

##### Examples

8P2 = 8 × 7 = 56
7P3 = 7 × 6 × 5 = 210


---

### C. Permutations of n Things Taken All at a Time

Number of permutations = n!


#### 1. Permutations of Similar Objects

If there are n objects of which:

* p1 are alike of one kind
* p2 are alike of another kind
* p3 are alike of third kind
* pr are alike of rth kind


such that:

p1 + p2 + p3 + … + pr = n


then number of permutations:

n! / (p1! × p2! × p3! × … × pr!)


---

### D. Combinations

#### 1. Definition

Each of the different groups or selections formed by taking some or all objects at a time is called a combination.

#### Examples

Out of three boys A, B, C selecting two:
(AB, BC, CA)

#### Note

AB and BA represent the same combination


The only combination of A, B, C taken all at a time is: ABC

Groups of two from A, B, C, D are: AB, AC, AD, BC, BD, CD


##### Important Note

AB and BA are different permutations,
but they represent the same combination

---

#### 2. Number of Combinations

Number of combinations of n things taken r at a time:

nCr = n! / [r! × (n − r)!]

#### Examples

8C3 = (8 × 7 × 6) / (3 × 2 × 1)
= 56

16C3 = (16 × 15 × 14) / (3 × 2 × 1)
= 560

---

#### 3. Important Properties


nCn = 1
nC0 = 1
nCr = nC(n − r)


##### Example Problems

```
1. In how many different ways can the letters of the word `"FIGHT"` be arranged?

Solution:

The word “FIGHT” contains 5 different letters
Required number of arrangements
= 5P5
= 5!
= 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
= 120

Therefore, the letters of the word `"FIGHT"` can be arranged in `120` different ways.



2. In how many different ways can the letters of the word `"DIGEST"` be arranged so that the vowels are never together?

Solution

The word “DIGEST” contains 6 different letters
Vowels are: I, E
First, find the number of arrangements in which the vowels are together
Treat IE as one letter
Then the letters become:
D, G, S, T, (IE)

Thus, there are 5 letters

Number of arrangements
= 5!
= 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
= 120

The vowels I and E can be arranged among themselves in 2! ways
= 2

Therefore,
Number of arrangements with vowels together
= 120 × 2
= 240

Now, Total number of arrangements of the word “DIGEST”
= 6!
= 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1
= 720

Therefore,
Number of arrangements in which vowels are never together
= 720 − 240
= 480

Hence, the required number of arrangements is `480`.
```

## Basic Concepts of Probability

Probability is defined as the likelihood of the occurrence of any event. It gives a numerical value to the chance or likelihood of something happening.

Probability is generally denoted by P(E), where E represents the event.

It is expressed as a number between 0 and 1:

* 0 → impossible event
* 1 → certain event
* 0 < P(E) < 1 → partial chance

### A. Formula for Probability

Probability of Event P(E)
= [Number of Favorable Outcomes] / [Total Number of Outcomes]

The probability of an event E, denoted by P(E), is a number between 0 and 1 that represents the likelihood of E occurring.

* If P(E) = 0 → impossible
* If P(E) = 1 → certain
* If 0 < P(E) < 1 → possible but not guaranteed

---

### B. Basic Probability Rules

#### Addition Rule

P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B)

##### Multiplication Rule for Independent Events

P(A∩B) = P(A) × P(B)

##### Complement Rule

P(A ′) = 1 - P(A)

---

### C. Probability Examples

```
1. There are 6 pillows in a bed, 3 are red, 2 are yellow, and 1 is blue. What is the probability of randomly picking a yellow pillow?

Solution:

Probability
= Number of yellow pillows / Total pillows
= 2/6
= 1/3


2. There is a container full of coloured bottles. Sumit performed an experiment `1000` times and got:

P(green)
= 450/1000

(b) If there are 100 bottles, how many are likely green?

450 out of 1000 are green

Therefore,
45 out of 100 are likely green
```
