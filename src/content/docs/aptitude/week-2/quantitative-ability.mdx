# Quantitative Ability - Week 2: Profit, Loss & Simple Interest

## Introduction

This week focuses on real-world applications of percentages through profit-loss scenarios and interest calculations - critical topics in competitive exams.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Profit and Loss

**Basic Definitions:**
- Cost Price (CP): Amount paid to buy an item
- Selling Price (SP): Amount received when selling
- Profit: SP - CP (where SP > CP)
- Loss: CP - SP (where CP > SP)

**Formulas:**
$$\text{Profit\%} = \frac{\text{SP} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$$

$$\text{Loss\%} = \frac{\text{CP} - \text{SP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$$

$$\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \frac{100 + \text{Profit\%}}{100}$$

### 2. Markup and Discount

**Markup (M.P - Marked Price):**
$$\text{Markup\%} = \frac{\text{M.P} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$$

**Discount:**
$$\text{Discount\%} = \frac{\text{M.P} - \text{SP}}{\text{M.P}} \times 100$$

**Successive Discounts:**
$$\text{Final Price} = \text{M.P} \times \frac{100 - D_1}{100} \times \frac{100 - D_2}{100}$$

### 3. Simple Interest

**Formula:**
$$\text{SI} = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$

Where:
- P = Principal
- R = Rate per annum
- T = Time in years
- A = P + SI (Amount)

### 4. Compound Interest

**Formula:**
$$A = P\left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T$$

$$\text{CI} = A - P$$

## Practice Problems

2. M.P = 1000, discount = 20%. Find SP
3. Successive discounts of 10% and 20%. Final price on 2000?
4. Principal = 5000, Rate = 5% p.a., Time = 4 years. Find SI and Amount

## Key Points to Remember

- Always use Cost Price as the base for profit/loss percentage
- Marked Price is used for discount calculations
- Simple interest is calculated annually on principal
- Compound interest increases exponentially with time
- Be careful with successive operations
