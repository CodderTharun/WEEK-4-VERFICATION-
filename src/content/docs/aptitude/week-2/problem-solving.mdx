# Multi-Variable Problems

## Introduction

This week introduces problems involving multiple variables, constraints, and relationships - essential for advanced problem-solving.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Problems with Multiple Constraints

**Approach:**
1. Define variables for unknowns
2. Write equations for each constraint
3. Solve the system of equations
4. Verify the solution against all constraints

**Example:**
- A + B = 10
- 2A + B = 16
- Solve: A = 6, B = 4

### 2. Optimization Problems

**Finding Maximum/Minimum:**
- Identify the objective (maximize or minimize)
- Note the constraints
- Use logical reasoning or algebra
- Verify solution is feasible

**Example:**
- Find max area of rectangle with perimeter = 20
- Answer: 5 × 5 (side = 5)

### 3. Rate and Distance Problems

**Key Formula:**
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$

**Types:**
- Relative speed (approaching/separating)
- Time to meet/overtake
- Average speed calculations

### 4. Age Relationship Problems

**Format:**
- Relationships between ages at different times
- Set up equations based on constraints
- Solve for unknowns

**Example:**
- Father is twice son's age now
- 10 years ago, father was 3 times son's age
- Find their current ages

## This Week's Topics

1. Solving systems of equations
2. Multi-constraint optimization
3. Rate, distance, and time problems
4. Age relationship problems
5. Real-world scenario problems

## Practice Problems

1. Two numbers sum to 30 and differ by 10. Find both.
2. A car travels 240 km at 60 km/h. How long did it take?
3. Father is 40 and son is 10 now. When was father 5 times older?
4. Working together, A and B complete a job in 4 hours. A alone needs 6 hours. How long for B alone?
5. Find the maximum product of two numbers whose sum is 24.

## Problem-Solving Template

**Step 1:** Define variables clearly
**Step 2:** Write all equations/constraints
**Step 3:** Solve systematically
**Step 4:** Check feasibility
**Step 5:** Verify against original problem

## Tips for Success

- Clearly define what each variable represents
- Write equations carefully
- Solve one step at a time
- Don't rush to the answer
- Always verify your solution
- Practice with diverse problem types
- Build confidence through consistent practice

Master multi-variable problem solving for excellence!
