#### LINE AND ANGLES

### A. Point


**The figure of which length, breadth and height cannot be measured is called a point. It is infinitesimal.**

### B. Line
A line is made up of an infinite number of points and it has only length i.e., it does not has any thickness (or width). A line is endless i.e., it can be extended in both directions.

### C. Line Segment

A line segment has two end points, but generally speaking line segment is called a line.   
A ---------------- B

(line segment AB)

### D. Ray

A ray extends indefinitely in one direction from any given point. This is exhibited by an arrow. The starting point is called as the initial point.  
O ─────────>

### E. Perpendicular Lines

Two lines intersecting each other and making a right angle, are called perpendicular lines.

Symbol: ⊥

### F. Concurrent Lines

More than two straight lines intersecting at the same point.

###  Example Problems

1.The base of a right angled triangle is 8 cm and hypotenuse is 17 cm. Find its area.
##### Solution
Hypotenuse = √[(base)² + (altitude)²]
17 = √(8² + x²)
289 = 64 + x²
x² = 225
x = 15 cm
∴ Area = ½ × base × altitude
(Height is called altitude also)
= ½ × 8 × 15
= 60 cm²
Answer: 60 cm²

2.Find the area of an equilateral triangle whose side is 4√3 cm.

##### Solution

Area of an equilateral triangle = (√3/4) × (side)²
= (√3/4) × (4√3)²
= (√3/4) × 48
= 12√3 cm²
Answer: 12√3 cm²

### Example Problems

1. The radius of a circular wheel is 1¾ m. How many revolutions will it make in travelling 11 km?
##### Solution
Total distance travelled = 11 km = 11000 m
Distance travelled in one revolution
= Circumference of the wheel
= 2 × π × r
= 2 × (22/7) × (7/4)
= 11 m
∴ Number of revolutions in 11 km
= 11000 / 11
= 1000 revolutions
Answer: 1000 revolutions.

 2. The radius of a circle is 5 m. What is the radius of another circle whose area is 25 times that of the first?

##### Solution
Ratio of areas = (ratio of radii)²

25/1 = (ratio of radii)²
⇒ Ratio of radii = 5/1
Therefore, the radius of the other circle is 5 times that of the first circle.
Hence, the required radius = 5 × 5 m
= 25 m
Answer: 25 m.

### A. RECTANGLES AND SQUARES

#### 1. Formulas

**Area of a rectangle**
= Length × Breadth
= l × b
**Area of a square**
= (side)²
= ½ (diagonal)²
= ½ d²
**Diagonal of a rectangle**
= √[(length)² + (breadth)²]
= √(l² + b²)
**Diagonal of a square**
= √(side² + side²)
= side √2
= a√2
**Perimeter of a rectangle**
= 2(length + breadth)
= 2(l + b)

**Perimeter of a square**
= 4 × side
= 4a

**Area of four walls**
= 2(length + breadth) × height
= 2(l + b) × h

#### 2. Example Problems

##### 1. Question: One side of a rectangular lawn is 12 m and its diagonal is 13 m. Find the area of the field.

**Solution:**
d = √(l² + b²)
13 = √(12² + b²)
169 = 144 + b²
b² = 25
b = 5 m
Area = l × b
= 12 × 5
= 60 m²
Answer: 60 m²

##### 2. Question: The length of a rectangle is 1 cm more than its breadth. The diagonal is 29 cm. Find the area of the rectangle.
a) 481 cm²
b) 841 cm²
c) 420 cm²
d) 870 m²

**Solution:**
Let breadth = b
Then length = (b + 1)
**Given,**
d = √(l² + b²)
29 = √((b + 1)² + b²)
Squaring both sides,
841 = (b + 1)² + b²
841 = b² + 2b + 1 + b²
840 = 2b² + 2b
420 = b² + b
b² + b − 420 = 0
(b + 21)(b − 20) = 0
b = 20
l = 21
Area = l × b
= 21 × 20
= 420 cm²
Answer: c) 420 cm²

= 6912 cm³
b) Surface Area

= 2(16 × 18 + 18 × 24 + 24 × 16)

**c) Diagonal**
Diagonal = √(l² + b² + h²)
= √(16² + 18² + 24²)
= √(256 + 324 + 576)
= √1156
= 34 cm

### C. Cylinder

#### Example Problems

##### 1. The base radius of a cylinder is 14 cm and its height is 30 cm. Find:

a) Volume
b) Curved surface area
c) Total surface area

**Solution:**
a) Volume
Volume of cylinder = πr²h
= (22/7) × 14 × 14 × 30
= 18480 cm³

**b) Curved Surface Area**
Curved surface area = 2πrh
= 2 × (22/7) × 14 × 30
= 2640 cm²

c) Total Surface Area
Total surface area = 2πr(h + r)
= 2 × (22/7) × 14 × (30 + 14)
= 2 × (22/7) × 14 × 44
= 3872 cm²

##### 2. How many cubic metres of earth must be dug to make a well 14 m deep and 4 m in diameter?

**Solution:**
Earth to be dug out from the well
= Volume of the cylindrical well
= πr²h
= (22/7) × 2 × 2 × 14
= 176 m³
Answer: 176 m³

##### 3. A hollow cylindrical tube open at both ends is made of iron 2 cm thick. If the external diameter is 50 cm and the length of the tube is 210 cm, find the number of cubic cm of iron in it.

**Solution:**
External radius (R) = 25 cm
Internal radius (r) = (25 − 2) = 23 cm

Volume of iron
= πh(R² − r²)
= (22/7) × 210 × (25² − 23²)
= (22/7) × 210 × (625 − 529)
= (22/7) × 210 × 96
= 63360 cm³
Answer: 63360 cm³ of iron.

### D. Sphere and Hemisphere

#### Example Problems

##### 1. Find the volume and surface area of a sphere of radius 3.5 cm.

**Solution:**
Volume = (4/3)πr³
= (4/3) × (22/7) × 3.5 × 3.5 × 3.5
= 179.66 cm³
Surface Area = 4πr²
= 4 × (22/7) × 3.5 × 3.5
= 154 cm²

##### 2. Find the volume, curved surface area and total surface area of a hemisphere of diameter 7 cm.

**Solution:**
Radius = 7/2 cm
**Volume**
Volume = (2/3)πr³
= (2/3) × (22/7) × (7/2) × (7/2) × (7/2)
= 89.833 cm³

**Curved Surface Area**
Curved surface area = 2πr²
= 2 × (22/7) × (7/2) × (7/2)
= 77 cm²
Total Surface Area
Total surface area = 3πr²
= 3 × (22/7) × (7/2) × (7/2)
= 115.5 cm²

##### 3. How many bullets can be made from a sphere of radius 8 cm? The radius of each bullet is 0.2 cm.

**Solution:**
**Number of bullets**
= (Volume of sphere) / (Volume of 1 bullet)
= [(4/3)π × 8³] / [(4/3)π × (0.2)³]
= 8³ / (0.2)³
= 512 / 0.008
= 64000
Answer: 64,000 bullets

##### 4. A sphere has the same curved surface area as a cone of height 12 cm and base radius 5 cm. Find the radius to the nearest cm.

**Solution:**
Slant height of cone
l = √(12² + 5²)
= √169
= 13 cm
Since the curved surface areas are equal,
4πr² = πrl
4πr² = π × 5 × 13
4r² = 65
r² = 65/4
r ≈ 4 cm
Answer: 4 cm (approx.)
