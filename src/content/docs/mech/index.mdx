# Mech


## Basic Thermodynamics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 1</strong></span> | Basic Thermodynamics Engineering Definitions and Concepts. Macroscopic and Microscopic Approaches. Thermodynamic Equilibrium and Quasi-static Processes. Zeroth Law and Measurement of Temperature. Work and Heat as Path Functions. First Law for Cyclic and Non-cyclic Processes. Steady Flow Energy Equation (SFEE) for Turbines, Compressors and Nozzles. Kelvin-Planck and Clausius Statements. Reversibility and Irreversibility. |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Thermodynamics: Carnot Cycle and Absolute Thermodynamic Temperature Scale, Clausius Inequality and Principle of Increase of Entropy, Entropy Generation in Closed and Open Systems, Properties of Pure Substances (P-V, P-T, and P-V-T Surfaces), Dryness Fraction Determination and Use of Steam Tables, Avogadro’s Law and Dalton’s Law of Partial Pressures, Vander Waal’s Equation and Compressibility Factor, Analog Modulation and Angle Modulation. |
