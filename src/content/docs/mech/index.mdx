## Basic Thermodynamics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 1</strong></span> | Basic Thermodynamics Engineering Definitions and Concepts. Macroscopic and Microscopic Approaches. Thermodynamic Equilibrium and Quasi-static Processes. Zeroth Law and Measurement of Temperature. Work and Heat as Path Functions. First Law for Cyclic and Non-cyclic Processes. Steady Flow Energy Equation (SFEE) for Turbines, Compressors and Nozzles. Kelvin-Planck and Clausius Statements. Reversibility and Irreversibility. |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Thermodynamics: Carnot Cycle and Absolute Thermodynamic Temperature Scale, Clausius Inequality and Principle of Increase of Entropy, Entropy Generation in Closed and Open Systems, Properties of Pure Substances (P-V, P-T, and P-V-T Surfaces), Dryness Fraction Determination and Use of Steam Tables, Avogadro’s Law and Dalton’s Law of Partial Pressures, Vander Waal’s Equation and Compressibility Factor, Analog Modulation and Angle Modulation. |

## Strength of Materials

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 1</strong></span> | Basic Engineering Definitions and Concepts. Stress-Strain Relations: Tension, Compression and Shear. Elastic Constants and Poisson's Ratio. Compound Bars and Extension/Shortening of Bars. Principal Planes and Principal Stresses/Strains. Mohr's Circle of Stresses and Strains. Temperature Stresses and Indeterminate Structural Problems. Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams (SFD and BMD). |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Bending Stresses and Radius of Curvature Relationships, Moment Carrying Capacity of Various Sections, Shearing Stresses in Beams, Theory of Pure Torsion and Power Transmitted, Torsional Rigidity and Stiffness of Shafts, Buckling of Columns, Critical Load and Effective Length. |

## Manufacturing Process

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Manufacturing Processes: Pattern Classification, Pattern Materials and Allowances, Green Sand, Core Sand, Shell and Investment Moulding, Gating and Riser Systems, Melting Furnaces (Cupola, Resistance and Induction), Die Casting (Gravity and Pressure) and Centrifugal Casting. Solidification Variables and Directional Solidification. Welding: Arc, Resistance and Special Welding (Laser and Electron Beam), Heat Affected Zone (HAZ) and Weld Metallurgy. Metal Cutting: Mechanics of Orthogonal and Oblique Cutting, Merchant Circle Diagram, Chip Formation, Tool Wear Mechanisms and Tool Life Equations. Milling: Simple, Compound and Differential Indexing Methods. Metal Forming: Hot and Cold Working, Forging (Drop and Press), Rolling (Angle of Bite), Drawing and Extrusion Processes. Sheet Metal Operations: Blanking, Piercing and Trimming. Iron Graph and Related Engineering Material Graphs. |

---

## Fluid Mechanics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Fluid Mechanics: Fluid Properties (Density, Specific Gravity, Viscosity, Surface Tension, Capillarity, Compressibility, Vapour Pressure), Newton’s Law of Viscosity and Types of Fluids (Ideal, Real, Newtonian and Non-Newtonian). Fluid Statics: Pressure Measurement, Manometers, Hydrostatic Forces, Centre of Pressure, Buoyancy, Archimedes Principle, Metacentre and Metacentric Height. Fluid Kinematics: Types of Flow, Reynolds Number and Continuity Equation. Fluid Dynamics: Bernoulli’s Equation, Euler’s Equation, Venturimeter, Orifice Meter, Pitot Tube, Discharge and Velocity Coefficients. Flow Through Pipes: Major and Minor Losses, Darcy-Weisbach Equation, Pipes in Series and Parallel, Water Hammer. Boundary Layer Theory: Laminar and Turbulent Boundary Layers, Displacement Thickness, Momentum Thickness, Flow Separation, Drag and Lift. Hydraulic Turbines: Pelton, Francis and Kaplan Turbines, Draft Tube and Cavitation. Pumps: Centrifugal and Reciprocating Pumps, Priming, NPSH and Cavitation. Important Interview Topics: Bernoulli’s Equation, Reynolds Number, Cavitation, Turbines, Pumps, Pipe Losses and Boundary Layer Theory. |

## Theory of Machines

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Theory of Machines: Power Transmission Drives – Belt Drives, Rope Drives and Chain Drives. Governors and Flywheel – Watt Governor, Porter Governor, Hartnell Governor, Sensitiveness, Stability, Hunting, Flywheel, Fluctuation of Energy and Coefficient of Fluctuation of Speed.
