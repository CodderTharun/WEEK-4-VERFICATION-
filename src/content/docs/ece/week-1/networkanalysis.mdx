#  Electrical Units and Measurement Conventions in DC Systems

##  Fundamental Electrical Quantities

- **Voltage ($V$ or $E$)**  
  Represents the potential energy required to move electrons between two points (nodes).  
  Measured in **Volts (V)**.

- **Current ($I$ or $i$)**  
  The continuous flow of electrical charge through a circuit.  
  Measured in **Amperes (A)**.

- **Resistance ($R$)**  
  The capacity of a material to resist or prevent the flow of electric charge.  
  Measured in **Ohms ($\Omega$)** and is always positive.

- **Conductance ($G$)**  
  The reciprocal of resistance, representing how easily current flows.  
  Measured in **Siemens (S)** or **mhos ($\mho$)**.  

  $$
  G = \frac{1}{R}
  $$

##  Ohm’s Law

At constant temperature, the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the voltage across it.

$$
V = IR
$$

$$
I = \frac{V}{R}
$$

$$
R = \frac{V}{I}
$$

---

##  Power Formulas

To calculate electrical power:

$$
P = VI
$$

$$
P = \frac{V^2}{R}
$$

$$
P = I^2 R
$$

---

##  Common DC Circuit Theory Terms

- **Circuit**  
  A closed loop conducting path in which electrical current flows.

- **Path**  
  A single line of connecting elements or sources.

- **Node**  
  A junction or connection point where two or more circuit elements meet.  
  Represented by a dot.

- **Branch**  
  A single component or group of components connected between two nodes.

- **Loop**  
  A simple closed path in a circuit where no element or node is encountered more than once.

- **Mesh**  
  A closed loop that does not contain any other loops inside it.

---

##  Kirchhoff’s Current Law (KCL)

Kirchhoff’s Current Law states that the total current entering a node is equal to the total current leaving the node.

**Principle:** Conservation of Charge  

$$
\sum I = 0
$$

$$
\sum I_{\text{in}} = \sum I_{\text{out}}
$$

## Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law (KVL)

Kirchhoff’s Voltage Law states that in any closed loop, the total voltage supplied is equal to the sum of all voltage drops.

**Principle:** Conservation of Energy  

$$
\sum V = 0
$$
