## Network Theory

| Week | Syllabus |
|---|---|
|<span style="white-space: nowrap;">**Week 1**</span> | Mesh Analysis. Nodal Analysis. KCL and KVL. Thevenin's Theorem. Norton's Theorem. Maximum Power Transfer Theorem. Two-Port Networks (XY and ABCD Parameters). Superposition Method. |

## Digital Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
|---|---|
| <span style="white-space: nowrap;">**Week 1**</span> | Introduction to Digital Electronics. Boolean Algebra & Logic: Rules, SOP & POS Conversion, Logic Gates. Number Systems & Conversions: Binary, Octal, Decimal, Hexadecimal, Arithmetic & Complements, Data Representation & Codes. K-Map Simplification. Combinational Circuits: Parity Generator, Adders, Subtractors, Multiplexer, Demultiplexer, Comparator, Encoder and Decoder. Sequential Circuits: Latches, Flip Flops, Counters and Shift Registers. Implementation of Complex Sequential Circuits. Verilog. |

---

## Analog Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Diodes, Transistors and IV Characteristics and Operations. Clipping and Clamping Circuits, Rectifiers and Other Electronic Circuits. Oscillators. Positive and Negative Feedback Concepts. Virtual Ground and Bandwidth Concepts. Frequency Domain Analysis. Active Filters. Amplifiers: Op-Amp, Class A, B, AB and Unit Gain Amplifiers. Applications of Op-Amps: Schmitt Trigger, 555 Timer. Two-Port Networks. |

---

## Computer Networks

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Introduction and Physical Layer: Definition of Computer Networks and Advantages. Network Devices: Router, Switch, Hub, Bridge, Gateway, Access Point, Modem, Firewall. Types of Networks: PAN, LAN, CAN, MAN, WAN. Internet and Protocols. OSI Model and TCP/IP Model. Network Topologies: Bus, Star, Ring, Mesh and Hybrid. Transmission Modes: Simplex, Half-Duplex and Full-Duplex. Transmission Media: Guided and Unguided Media. Data Link Layer: Services, Basic Design Issues and Framing Techniques. Framing Methods: Byte Count, Bit Stuffing and Byte Stuffing. Error and Flow Control: Error Detection using Parity, CRCs and Checksum. Error Correction: Hamming Codes, Binary Convolutional Codes, Reed-Solomon Codes and Low-Density Parity Check Codes. Sliding Window Protocols: Go-Back-N and Selective Repeat. Medium Access Sublayer: Channel Allocation Problems, MAC Protocols, ALOHA, Carrier Sense Protocols and Collision-Free Protocols. Network Layer: Functions of Network Layer, IP Addressing (IPv4 & IPv6), Routing, Routing Tables, Static Routing, Dynamic Routing, Default Routing, Distance Vector Routing, Link State Routing, Routing Protocols (OSPF, IS-IS, EIGRP, BGP), Network Layer Protocols: IP, ICMP, ARP, RARP, NAT, IPSec and MPLS. Transport Layer: TCP Features, Three-Way Handshake, TCP Header Fields, UDP Features, Difference between TCP and UDP, Congestion Control, Leaky Bucket Algorithm and Token Bucket Algorithm. Application Layer: Functions of Application Layer, Client-Server Model, Working of Application Layer, Protocols: HTTP/HTTPS, DNS, TELNET, DHCP, FTP, SMTP, NFS, SNMP, POP, IRC, MIME, NNTP, LDAP, NTP, MQTT and SIP. |

## Verilog

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Introduction to Verilog HDL: HDL, Typical Design Flow, Hierarchical Modeling, Top-Down and Bottom-Up Design Methodologies. Verilog HDL Concepts: Module, Ports, Instance, Module Instantiation, Levels of Abstraction. Data Types, Operands, Operators, Signal Assignment, Concurrent and Sequential Constructs, System Tasks and Compiler Directives. Modeling in Verilog HDL: Gate Level Modeling, Gate Delays, User Defined Primitives (UDP), Dataflow Modeling, Continuous Assignment and Delays. Behavioral Modeling: Initial and Always Blocks, Procedural Assignments, Timing Controls, Conditional Statements, Case Statements and Loops. Structural Modeling and Hardware Modeling: Parameter Ports, Module Instantiation, External Ports, Generate Statements, Configurations, Tasks and Functions, System Tasks and Functions, Disable Statement, Named Events, Synchronous Logic, Moore and Mealy FSM. Logic Synthesis and FPGA: Logic Synthesis, Design Partitioning, PLDs, PROM, PAL, PLA, CPLDs, FPGA Architecture and PSoC. |

## Analog and Digital Communication

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Principles of analog modulation techniques including AM, spectrum analysis, power calculations, modulation and detection methods, AM systems such as DSB-SC, SSB, ring modulators and coherent detection, Noise concepts including thermal noise, shot noise, SNR and noise figure, Angle modulation techniques such as FM, PM, NBFM and WBFM along with bandwidth and demodulation, Noise in AM and FM receivers including pre-emphasis and de-emphasis methods, Digital communication concepts including sampling, PAM, TDM, PCM and quantization, Baseband transmission techniques such as line coding, ISI, eye pattern and equalization, and Digital modulation schemes including BPSK, QPSK, DPSK, BFSK, M-PSK and QAM. |
