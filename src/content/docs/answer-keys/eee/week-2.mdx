## Question 1

1. The speed of a boat when travelling downstream is 32 km/hr, whereas when travelling upstream it is 28 km/hr. What is the speed of the boat in still water and the speed of the stream?

- A) 30 km/hr, 2 km/hr
- B) 28 km/hr, 4 km/hr
- C) 32 km/hr, 2 km/hr
- D) 30 km/hr, 4 km/hr

Correct Answer: A) 30 km/hr, 2 km/hr

Explanation:

Downstream speed = 32 km/hr

Upstream speed = 28 km/hr

Speed of boat in still water

= (32 + 28) / 2

= 60 / 2

= 30 km/hr

Speed of stream

= (32 − 28) / 2

= 4 / 2

= 2 km/hr

## Question 14

14. The value of k for which the quadratic equation kx(x − 2) + 6 = 0 has equal roots is:

- A) 2
- B) 3
- C) 6
- D) −6

Correct Answer: C) 6

Explanation:

Given equation

kx(x − 2) + 6 = 0

⇒ kx² − 2kx + 6 = 0

Comparing with ax² + bx + c = 0,

a = k

b = −2k

c = 6

For equal roots,

b² − 4ac = 0

⇒ (−2k)² − 4(k)(6) = 0

⇒ 4k² − 24k = 0

⇒ 4k(k − 6) = 0

⇒ k = 0 or k = 6

Since k ≠ 0 for a quadratic equation,

k = 6

15. Quadratic equation corresponding to the roots 2 + √5 and 2 − √5 is:

- A) x² − 4x − 1 = 0
- B) x² + 4x − 1 = 0
- C) x² − 2x − 1 = 0
- D) x² − 4x + 1 = 0

Correct Answer: A) x² − 4x − 1 = 0

Explanation:

Given roots:

α = 2 + √5

β = 2 − √5

Sum of roots

= α + β

= (2 + √5) + (2 − √5)

= 4

Product of roots

= αβ

= (2 + √5)(2 − √5)

= 4 − 5

= −1

Quadratic equation

= x² − (sum of roots)x + (product of roots)

= 0

⇒ x² − 4x − 1 = 0

16. Why does a gradient occur in a semiconductor?

- A) Because of current flow
- B) Because of diffusion current
- C) Because of drift current
- D) Because of difference in concentrations

Correct Answer: D) Because of difference in concentrations

Explanation:

## Question 17

17. A uniformly doped semiconductor bar is subjected to an electric field directed along the +x axis. Electron concentration is also higher at the left end than the right end. Which statement is correct regarding electron drift and diffusion currents?

- A) Both drift and diffusion currents are along +x direction
- B) Drift current is along −x and diffusion current is along +x
- C) Drift current is along +x and diffusion current is along −x
- D) Both drift and diffusion currents are along −x direction

Correct Answer: A) Both drift and diffusion currents are along +x direction

Explanation:

Electrons move opposite to the applied electric field.

Since the electric field is along +x, electron motion is toward −x.

Conventional current due to electron drift is opposite to electron motion and therefore flows along +x.

For diffusion, electrons move from the region of high concentration on the left toward the region of low concentration on the right.

The resulting diffusion current is also along +x.

Therefore, both drift and diffusion currents are in the same direction.

## Question 20

20. A diode has a reverse saturation current Is. According to the Shockley equation, when the applied voltage is large and negative, the diode current approximately becomes:

- A) +Is
- B) 0
- C) −Is
- D) Infinite

Correct Answer: C) −Is

Explanation:

The Shockley diode equation is:

I = Is(e^(V/ηVT) − 1)

For a large negative voltage,

e^(V/ηVT) ≈ 0

Therefore,

I ≈ Is(0 − 1)

I ≈ −Is

Hence the diode current approaches the reverse saturation current.

- A) Avalanche breakdown
- B) Thermal breakdown
- C) Zener breakdown
- D) Diffusion breakdown

26. Why are power semiconductor devices mainly operated in switching mode?

- B) To maximize power loss
- C) To minimize power dissipation
- D) To operate continuously in linear region

Correct Answer: C) To minimize power dissipation

Explanation:

As a result, power loss is minimized and efficiency is improved.

## Question 27

27. A diode with large reverse recovery time is used in a high-frequency SMPS circuit. Which effect is MOST likely?

- A) Reduced switching losses
- B) Improved efficiency
- C) Increased EMI noise and heating
- D) Zero reverse current

Correct Answer: C) Increased EMI noise and heating

Explanation:

A large reverse recovery time causes the diode to continue conducting briefly after switching.

This results in higher switching losses, increased heat generation, greater electromagnetic interference (EMI), and reduced efficiency.

Therefore, fast recovery diodes are preferred in SMPS and inverter applications.

## Question 35

35. In a boost chopper, why can the output voltage become greater than the input voltage?

- A) The duty cycle becomes negative
- B) Energy stored in the inductor during ON state is released during OFF state
- C) The input DC source reverses polarity
- D) The switch remains permanently ON

Correct Answer: B) Energy stored in the inductor during ON state is released during OFF state

Explanation:

In a boost converter, the inductor stores energy when the switch is ON.
