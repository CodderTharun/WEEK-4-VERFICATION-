## C Programming

| Week | Syllabus |
|---|---|
| <span style="white-space: nowrap;">**Week 1**</span> | Variables and Data Types. Operators and Expressions. Conditional Statements (`if`, `switch`). |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Loops (for, while, do-while). Functions and Recursion. Arrays and Strings. Pointers. Structures and Unions. Bitwise Operators. File Handling. |

## Network Theory

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 1</strong></span> | KCL and KVL. Mesh Analysis. Nodal Analysis. Thevenin's Theorem. Norton's Theorem. Maximum Power Transfer Theorem. Two-Port Networks (XY and ABCD Parameters). Superposition Method. Network Topology. Transient Behaviour. |

---

## Digital Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 1</strong></span> | Digital Electronics & Signals. Number System & Basic Terminology. Binary Arithmetic & Complements. Binary Codes & Conversions. Boolean Algebra, Expressions & K-Map. Logic Gates & Universal Gates. Combinational Circuits (Adders, Subtractors, Decoder, Encoder, MUX, DEMUX). Sequential Circuits (Latches, Flip-Flops, Registers, Counters). |

---

## Analog Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Semiconductor. PN Junction Diode. Clipping and Clamping Circuits. Rectifiers and Power Supplies. Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT). |

---

## Electrical Machines

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | DC Machines (Construction, Working, Types). DC Generators & Motors (EMF, Back EMF, Applications). Losses & Performance of DC Machines. Induction Motors (AC Machines) (Construction, Working, Types). Induction Motor Characteristics & Speed Control. Synchronous Machines & Alternators. EMF Equation, Armature Reaction & Performance. Transformers (Working, Types, Applications). |

---

## Power Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 2</strong></span> | Power Electronics Basics & Applications. Power Semiconductor Devices (Diode, SCR, TRIAC, MOSFET, IGBT). Device Characteristics & Protection (V-I, Switching, di/dt, dv/dt, Recovery). Thyristor Control & Firing Circuits. Rectifiers (Uncontrolled & Controlled). Inverters. Choppers (DC–DC Converters). AC Controllers & Cycloconverters. |

## Analog Electronics

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 3</strong></span> | Amplifiers. Feedback Amplifiers. Oscillators. Operational Amplifiers (Op-Amps). Applications of Op-Amps. Frequency Domain Analysis. Two-Port Networks. Analog VLSI. |

## Microprocessors and Embedded Systems

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 3</strong></span> | Introduction to Microprocessors and Their Generations. Microprocessor Architecture and Components. Instruction Cycle and Instruction Set Architecture (ISA). Programming Model and Addressing Modes. Assembly Language Programming. Microcontrollers and Embedded Systems (8051). Memory Organization, Interrupts, and Data Transfer Techniques (DMA, Serial Communication). Interfacing and System Organization including I/O, Memory, Bus Architecture, Stack, and Flag Registers. |

---

## Electric Vehicles

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Electric Vehicles (EVs): Introduction to Electric Vehicles, Types, Advantages and Challenges. Energy Storage Systems: Batteries, Fuel Cells and Supercapacitors. Electric Propulsion Systems: Electric Motors and Drive Systems. Power Electronics in EVs: Converters and Inverters. Battery Management System (BMS). Charging Infrastructure and Charging Methods. Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs): Types and Operation. Energy Efficiency and Control Strategies in Electric Vehicles. |

---

## Power Systems

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Power Systems: Power System Overview and Components (Generation, Transmission and Distribution). Transmission Line Parameters and Modeling. Performance of Transmission Lines: Efficiency and Voltage Regulation. Power Flow Analysis. Economic Operation of Power Systems: Load Dispatch and Unit Commitment. Fault Analysis in Power Systems. Protection Systems and Relays. Stability of Power Systems. |

---

## VLSI

| Week | Syllabus |
| --- | --- |
| <span style={{ whiteSpace: 'nowrap' }}><strong>Week 4</strong></span> | Digital VLSI: Basics of MOSFETs, MOSFET Operation, I-V Characteristics and Second-Order Effects, Static Characteristics, Combinational MOS Circuits (NAND, NOR and Transmission Logic Circuits), Sequential MOS Circuits (Latches and Bistable Elements), Dynamic Logic, Timing Issues and Semiconductor Memory.    Analog VLSI: Fundamentals of Analog VLSI Design, Single-Stage Amplifiers, Differential Amplifiers and Differential Pair Analysis, Operational Amplifiers and Their Applications, Capacitor Circuits and Switched-Capacitor Techniques, Oscillators and Waveform Generation Circuits, and Phase-Locked Loops (PLLs) for Communication and Signal Processing Systems. |
